Is Reiki for me?

Reiki isn't the simple answer to all your problems. The benefits of Reiki can range from helping with stress, insomnia, trauma, chronic pain to energy blockages, fatigue, foggy brain, depression, however there's more. Reiki can also shake your life, your habits, your energy level, your mood, your ability to say no. Most importantly, Reiki is a non-invasive, non-addictive energy healing technique that is a beautiful complement to any other kind of treatment (traditional medicine, naturopathy, nutrition, counselling, osteopathy, remedial massage etc).


How does Reiki feel?

Reiki can be translated from the Japanese, where it traditionally comes from, to “spiritually guided life force energy”. During a session, Reiki practitioners act as a channel, tuning into this energy and guiding it in your body. Receiving Reiki can be quite an experience, the recipient usually feels warmth and tingling, and then leaves the session feeling relaxed.

Is Yoga for me?

Originating from India, The word Yoga comes from the Sanskrit “Yoke”, meaning “union”. The physical practice, commonly shared in the West, serves as a preparation to sit. Once the body has moved, the mind can find quietness.
Breathing exercises, mantra recitations and different meditations are all part of a Yoga practice so Yoga is accessible to everyone. The aim is to calm the fluctuation of the mind by uniting body and mind.


How does Yoga feel?

Exploring shapes the body can make can range from intimidating to exhilarating. During a practice, the invitation is to enquire how the mind is reacting, learning to unravel negative habits and rather fall into the awareness of the present moment.
Mindfulness, the gift of Yoga, is then practiced outside of the mat and leads to better stress management, focus, productivity and beautiful connection to self & others.


Managing expectations

When booking for a private or a group session, I focus on a few things:
* accessibility (experience, age, gender, ability...): we workshop what works best for you.
* agency / trauma informed: with invitations and guidance, you are in control of your practice. Hands-on modifications are always optional.
* cultural appreciation: learning and sharing all aspects of Yoga from its roots, mythology and different tools & practices.
